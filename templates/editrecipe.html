Completing the Add a Recipe Form

  1. Summary Section
    2. Cuisine type
    You can choose to select from the dropdown list a cuisine type or you could add a new cuisine by clicking on the Add Cuisine button besides it. This will redirect you to a simple form where you will be required to type in the cuisine type you wish to create and a summary of the cuisine.
    Recipe Name
    This is the name you would like to call the recipe you are creating. If your recipe has been set to make recipe public, then this is the name that everyone will see on the site.
    Recipe Summary
    This is a summary for the recipe you are creating to give users an idea of what your recipe is about and why they should have a go to try it. Should be a maximum of 95 characters.
    Serves
    Serves refers to the number of people the recipe will cater for regarding the size of the food to be cooked. For example a serves of 5 means the recipe ingredients are enough to feed up to 5 people.
    Not sure what Allergens your recipe may have?
    The allergens listed on the dropdown list of this Add a Recipe form have been taken from the main 14 allergens in ingredients according to the UK Food Standards Agency.
    Gluten Free Checkbox
    A gluten-free diet is a diet that excludes the protein gluten. Gluten is found in grains such as wheat, barley, rye, and a cross between wheat and rye called triticale. If your recipe is gluten free, tick this box before submitting.
    Suitable for Vegetarians Checkbox
    A Vegetarian is someone who does not eat meat or fish, and sometimes other animal products, especially for moral, religious, or health reasons. If your recipe is suitable for vegetarians, tick this box before submitting.
    Recipe Image URL
    In order to add an appropriate recipe image URL, we advise using google images to find your image. First, make a search for a suitable image on google, then click on the image to be redirected to the website it is from. Next, drag the image from the website into the current tab window and you should be redirected to a page which just displays the image. Copy the link that appears on that page, it should end with .jpg/.jpeg/.png. Paste this link into the Recipe Image URL field.
  2. Ingredients Section
    3. Ingredient Name
    This is the name of one item of ingredient for your recipe. Once you begin typing in your ingredient name, more input fields for ingredients will appear below. Maximum number of ingredients is 20.
    Ingredient Quantity
    This is the quantity of the item of ingredient for your recipe that you mentioned on the left.
    Ingredient Units
    This is the quantity units of the item of ingredient for your recipe that you mentioned on the left. If no units are required for an ingredient, do not select any of the units in the dropdown.
  3. Method Section
    4. Prep Time
    Prep Time is short for Preparation Time. This is the time you advise users to prepare things before you actually begin cooking, it could include for example slicing onions and washing vegetables. A couple of minutes will be an ideal prep time.
    Cook Time
    This is the time you advise users that completing the recipe will take, this includes the completion of all the method steps listed. A recipe cook time shouldn't be too long as this is likely to make it unpopular, recipe cook times must be realistic and appropriate.
    Method Steps
    Maximum number of steps is 10. Place each method step in chronological order, with the first step on 1. These should be clear instructions for a user to follow to successfully cook the meal.
  4. What happens if I make my recipe public?
    5. If you make your recipe public, everyone who visits the site will see your recipe among the other recipes made public on OneTwoCook. On our website, we display your recipe along with your name and region at the bottom of your recipe's page. We do not display date of births, as this information is used for user identification purposes only and will not be shared with third parties.
